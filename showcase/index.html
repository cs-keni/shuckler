About

Built for the music listener,
not the casual user.

Shuckler started as a personal project to solve a simple problem: most music apps treat your library as a subscription, not a collection. You don't own your music in Spotify. Your listening history disappears when you change apps. The UI is designed to sell you things, not to help you listen.

Shuckler is different. You download songs directly from YouTube — they're yours, stored locally, playable offline forever. Your listening history is tracked on your device. Your taste is visible to you, not to an algorithm.

The design reflects this philosophy: warm, personal, and made to feel like it was built by hand. DM Serif Display for the moments that matter. Album art shown at true aspect ratio. A color system that adapts to whatever you're playing.

Design Philosophy

Seven principles.

Every visual and interaction decision in Shuckler traces back to one of these.

01
Music moves the UI
Audio amplitude drives subtle animations — not decorations bolted on top.
02
Rectangles, not circles
Music lives in rectangles: album covers, cassette inserts, jewel cases. Circles crop the artist's composition.
03
Warmth over neutral
#0C0A07, not #000000. A whisper of warmth makes the app feel like listening under a lamp.
04
Gestures are first-class
Expand, dismiss, skip — via continuous gestures. The UI feels physically held.
05
The DM type system
DM Serif Display (emotional) + DM Mono (data). Same type family — intentional, cohesive.
06
Art as the only accent
Album Palette tints every surface. An advantage no commercial streaming service can copy.
07
Your listening, visible
Stats and scrobbling make the app feel like a living record of your taste.

Features

Everything it does.

Shuckler is built around features that matter to people who actually care about their music.

Design
Dynamic Album Colors
The Palette API extracts the dominant color from every album's artwork and tints the entire UI — progress bars, amplitude visualizers, surface overlays. Every album has its own feel.
Feature
Last.fm Scrobbling
Connect your Last.fm account in Settings. Every song played past 50% is automatically scrobbled — your listening history lives on your profile forever, independent of any app.
Feature
Listening Stats
Every play is recorded locally. The Stats screen shows weekly listening hours, a day-by-day chart, and your top artists — no cloud required, no account needed.
Feature
Smart Auto-Playlists
Generated automatically from your listening history: Most Played, Recently Added, Long Sessions, Hidden Gems. Your habits become playlists — no curation needed.
Design
Floating Pill Player
A compact floating pill replaces the traditional bottom bar. Swipe up to expand to full Now Playing. The pill shows live amplitude bars and a song progress line at all times.
Feature
Waveform Downloads
Downloads don't show a boring progress bar — they show a waveform that fills left-to-right as bytes arrive, with an amber frontier bar at the leading edge.
Feature
Line-Synced Lyrics
Open the lyrics view from Now Playing. The currently playing line is highlighted in DM Serif Display at a larger size; surrounding lines fade in proportion to their distance from the present moment.
Feature
Spotify Metadata
Connect Spotify to silently enrich your library with artist photos and biographies — no extra downloads, no manual curation. Artist pages get richer over time.
Interaction Design

Gesture-first.

Every core action can be done with a gesture. The app responds as if physically held — not tapped.

↑
Swipe up pill
Expands to Now Playing with a shared-element transition on the album art
↓
Swipe down
Collapses Now Playing — art springs back to pill thumbnail size
←→
Swipe album card
Left = next track, right = previous. Card tilts with physics then snaps away
⟳
Long press track
Context menu: add to queue, download, add to playlist, share
↔
Scrub progress
Drag on the progress bar — thumb springs to your finger position
↕
Reorder queue
Long press + drag queue rows with spring physics and haptic on lift
